Oyako Don Recipe

Description

Oyako don is the result of a parent and child's hard work. "Oya" translates to parent, and "ko" to child, and in this dish, chicken meat and egg respectively.

Served over a bed of rice, oyakodon is ultra satifying and unbelievabely simple to make. With just a handful of ingredients, this can cooked and on your table in less than 30 minutes (give or take a few minutes based on rice cooking time.

Ingredients

  • Chicken thigh
  • 2 Eggs
  • Sliced onion
  • Granulated dashi
  • Soy sauce
  • Mirin
  • Cooking sake
  • Sugar
  • Shredded seaweed
  • Rice

Steps

  1. Combine rice and water into a rice cooker.
  2. Slide chicken thigh meat into bite-sized chunks.
  3. Combine water, granulated dashi, soy sauce, mirin, cooking sake, and sugar in a small sauce pan and cook over low heat until slighly reduced
  4. Crack two eggs into a bowl and beat until mixed. Set aside.
  5. In a 5 inch saute pan, add the ground chicken, sliced onion, and 150 ml of the reduced sauce from step 3. Cook over high heat, stir and turn over occasionally.
  6. Once chicken is cooked through, onion is softened, and sauce reduced by 1/3, pour beaten eggs into saute pan. Pour in circle starting from outer edge of pan, working your way to the center. Reduce heat to low.
  7. Scoop roughly 200 grams of rice into bowl and smooth the top to create a flat surface.
  8. Spoon over egg and chicken mixture, topp with a pinch of seaweed, and serve hot.
